Saturday RRHS mid-day events

10a Parade and Time Capsule

Centennial/Masons parade along Hamilton Street celebrating our 100th and the Cornerstone time capsule reveal. Cornerstone ceremony assisted by Masons - A time capsule was cemented by the Masons in 1921 in the northern-most corner of the RRHS senior building. We've waited 100 years. You do NOT want to miss the unveiling of the time capsule. The Masons will be on hand to assist in the ceremony of our 100-year-old capsule. The Masons will perform a 100-year re-dedication ritual.

Saturday gala 7p-11pm

Buy Tickets
Sponsored by RRHS Alumni & Friends Association. You do not want to miss this once-in-a-hundred-years gala event. Cost is $55 per person. You may purchase tickets using the button above. Tickets may also be purchased in person at the office of G.W. Hux Insurance in Roanoke Rapids.

Dine on heavy hors d'oeuvres catered by David Watson as you listen to music and enjoy the company of fellow alums. Be sure to bid on some fantastic items at our silent auction and purchase 50/50 raffle tickets. The proceeds will benefit the RRHS Alumni & Friends Association and some of our RRHS student organizations. Cash bar.

Event will be both inside and outside. Large tents will be outside the Civic Center for extra areas for guests to comfortably socialize.

Treasure Hunt - $1000 prize

Treasure Hunt sponsored by Roanoke Rapids Alumni & Friends Association. City-wide treasure hunt begins Sept 24th. A gold-colored dog tag necklace has been hidden in the Roanoke Rapids area as the treasure. The finder of the treasure wins $1000 from the Alumni Association. A riddle is provided in increments to help you find the necklace. The first clue of the riddle will be released Friday Sept 24th at 8am. Additional clues of the riddle will be released incrementally on a schedule. The treasure is NOT on school property. The treasure is on publicly accessible property.

Bows and Paintings

Bows: Beginning Wednesday, August 18 - Local residents and businesses are encouraged to purchase and display an official RRHS Centennial Bow from Exquisite Occasions, 1025 East Tenth Street for $10. All proceeds benefit RRHS Building Preservation.
Painting:Purchase the the official Centennial water color painting or ornament of the historic high school by North Carolina artist Lew Wilson. Both can be customized with your graduation year. A portion of the proceeds benefit student programs at RRHS.
